# Launch Copy Support Sheet — Growth Handoff Format

> **Artifact status:** **CURRENT**
> This support sheet is the active launch-support copy artifact for growth use.
>
> **Superseded artifacts:** Any earlier draft messaging framework, exploratory branch doc, or interim launch copy note not marked **CURRENT** should be treated as reference only and not used as source copy.

## 1) What This Sheet Is For
- A compact handoff doc for growth to pull launch-ready language fast.
- Built for landing pages, waitlist prompts, share prompts, signup surfaces, lightweight ads, and social support copy.
- Meant to reduce improvising. If a line is here, use it as written or trim lightly without changing tone.
- Priority: clarity first, warmth second, cleverness third.

## 2) Core Messaging Guardrails
- Keep the product feeling **useful, lively, and human**.
- Sound **witty, warm, and specific** — never vague, grandiose, or “future of everything.”
- Prefer plainspoken verbs over inflated claims.
- Keep copy concrete: what it does, why it helps, why it feels good to use.
- Avoid stock launch language like:
  - “revolutionary”
  - “game-changing”
  - “seamless solution”
  - “built for teams of all sizes”
  - “unlock your potential”
- Do not oversell certainty, scale, or outcomes the product cannot prove.
- If choosing between clever and clear, choose clear.

## 3) Approved Copy Zones
- **Headline zone:** short, clean, ownable; one idea per line.
- **Subhead zone:** explain the value in plain English; no jargon stack.
- **CTA zone:** direct and low-friction.
  - Examples of acceptable construction:
    - Join the waitlist
    - Get early access
    - Save my spot
    - Share with a friend
- **Supporting microcopy zone:** reassuring, light, helpful.
  - Examples:
    - No essay required.
    - Takes less than a minute.
    - We’ll let you know when it’s live.
- **Share language zone:** make it feel social, not transactional.
  - Emphasize invitation over referral mechanics.
  - Keep the tone generous, not hustle-heavy.

## 4) Tone Calibration: Do / Don’t
- **Do**
  - Be concise.
  - Use specific language people can picture.
  - Leave a little personality in the sentence.
  - Write like a smart human talking to another human.
  - Let confidence come from precision, not hype.
- **Don’t**
  - Sound like a pitch deck.
  - Stack adjectives where one noun will do.
  - Use startup filler or “category-defining” language.
  - Turn every line into a punchline.
  - Write copy that could belong to any app on the internet.

## 5) Handoff Notes for Growth
- Treat this as the **current** copy-support artifact until leadership says otherwise.
- If another doc conflicts with this one, this one wins unless a newer artifact is explicitly marked **CURRENT**.
- Use this sheet to keep downstream variants aligned across channels.
- Small edits for fit are fine; tone drift is not.
- When adapting:
  - shorten before rewriting
  - simplify before embellishing
  - keep the product voice recognizable
