## 1) Narrative architecture

### Brand frame
**APPROVED**

- **Product name:** Pika
- **Category frame:** instant-restart social game
- **Core promise:** less waiting, more rematches
- **Personality standard:** witty, warm, specific
- **Voice rule:** crisp over clever
- **Voice rule:** warm, not chatty
- **Voice rule:** specific, not theatrical
- **Voice rule:** never pretend certainty the product cannot prove

### Core launch narrative
**APPROVED**

Pika is built for the moment when a game ends and nobody wants the energy to drop. Instead of making people rebuild the room, re-explain the rules, or hunt for the next match, Pika keeps the group moving. It gets players from finished round to next round fast, with less friction and less dead air.

### Short narrative variants
**APPROVED**

- **One-line:** Pika keeps game night in motion.
- **Two-line:** The round ends. The momentum doesn’t have to. Pika is the social game flow built for instant restarts and fast rematches.
- **Three-line:** Most games lose people between rounds. Pika is designed to keep the room warm, the next matchup close, and the restart easy. Less reset. More playing.

### Tension / solution framing
**APPROVED**

- **Tension:** group energy drops during setup, reshuffling, and restart friction
- **Product answer:** fast room flow, quick regrouping, and instant-restart structure
- **Emotional payoff:** the night keeps moving

### Audience framing
**PROVISIONAL**

- **Primary audience:** friends playing casual social games together in shared physical or chat-based spaces
- **Secondary audience:** hosts who want lower-friction replays and cleaner room coordination
- **Open variable:** whether launch messaging should speak more to hosts, all players, or both equally

## 6) Claim guardrails

### Safe claims
**APPROVED**

Use these without extra substantiation, assuming the product experience still matches them:

- Pika is built for instant restarts.
- Pika is designed to reduce restart friction.
- Pika keeps players moving from one round to the next.
- Pika is made for fast rematches.
- Pika is designed for replay-friendly group flow.

### Claims requiring proof before external use
**PROVISIONAL**

Do not publish externally until evidence exists and legal/product stakeholders agree:

- Any statement with numbers, percentages, timing, rankings, or superlatives
- “Fastest”
- “Best”
- “Most social”
- “Everyone”
- “Never”
- “No setup”
- “Zero friction”
- “Players stay longer”
- “Improves retention”
- “Cuts restart time by X%”
- “Loved by hosts”
- “Designed for every game night”

### Language to avoid
**APPROVED**

- Corporate filler: seamless, robust, best-in-class, frictionless ecosystem
- Hype inflation: revolutionary, game-changing, category-defining
- Fake certainty: guaranteed, always, never fails
- Stock-SaaS phrasing: empower your users, unlock synergies, optimize engagement

### Confidence-language rules
**APPROVED**

- If it is observed but unmeasured, say “designed to,” “built to,” or “meant to.”
- If it is not confirmed, do not imply certainty.
- If a feature or launch detail may change, mark it PROVISIONAL.
- If a proof point is missing, use a placeholder and keep the surrounding copy qualitative.

## 8) Usage notes

### External usage rules
**APPROVED**

- Use APPROVED copy as written or with minor length edits only.
- Keep the central promise focused on momentum, restart ease, and replay flow.
- Do not invent quantified proof.
- Do not imply features, rewards, timelines, or access policies that are not confirmed.
- Prefer one strong promise over three vague ones.

### Internal editing rules
**APPROVED**

- If a line gets punchier but less precise, reject it.
- If a line sounds generic enough to fit any startup, reject it.
- If a line needs a footnote to stop being misleading, downgrade it to PROVISIONAL or rewrite it.
- Keep copy specific to social play, replay energy, and rematch behavior.

### Swap-ready placeholder rules
**APPROVED**

- Placeholders must be bracketed like `[this]`
- Any placeholder attached to a claim keeps the whole block PROVISIONAL until replaced
- Never remove the approval label when adapting this doc into decks or pages
- If a PROVISIONAL block is promoted, update the label in the downstream asset too

### Scope freeze
**APPROVED**

This document covers launch-prep messaging only: narrative, deck structure, waitlist/share language, claims, and proof handling. It does not approve product UI copy outside launch materials, visual design, pricing language, legal claims, or roadmap promises.
