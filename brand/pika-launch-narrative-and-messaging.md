---

## Launch narrative

Making plans with other people should not require a part-time project manager.

But that is somehow where group decisions end up: too many options, too many opinions, too many non-answers, and one heroic person trying to turn “maybe” into an actual plan. The result is familiar: long threads, fading enthusiasm, and a lot of “wait, what are we even doing?”

Pika exists for that exact moment.

It takes the fuzzy, repetitive, mildly chaotic process of deciding as a group and turns it into something quick and usable. Instead of letting the conversation sprawl forever, Pika helps people gather options, react clearly, and move toward an actual choice.

The point is not to make decision-making feel formal. The point is to make it feel possible.

Pika is for friends trying to pick dinner, roommates sorting out weekend plans, teams choosing a time, or anyone trapped in the ancient curse of “I’m down for whatever.” It brings enough structure to get people unstuck, without sucking the fun out of making plans together.

The emotional win is simple: less circling, more doing.

## Three-message hierarchy

### 1) Core message
**Pika helps groups decide faster.**

If a conversation is looping, scattered, or full of non-committal replies, Pika gives the group a cleaner path to a real decision.

### 2) Supporting message
**It turns messy group chat energy into a visible next step.**

People can stop guessing what everyone means, stop rereading the thread, and stop waiting for someone else to take charge.

### 3) Emotional message
**Planning together feels lighter when the decision finally moves.**

Pika reduces the tiny social friction that makes group plans drag on. It helps people go from vague interest to actual momentum.

## Audience framing

### Primary audience
Friends, roommates, and small groups who regularly make casual plans together and are tired of chaotic decision threads.

### Secondary audience
Organizers-by-default: the person who always has to ask again, summarize options, chase replies, and convert soft maybes into a real plan.

### Audience truth
Most group decisions do not fail because people disagree dramatically. They fail because nobody wants to be annoying, nobody wants to over-own the plan, and nobody can tell when a choice is actually made.

### Audience payoff
Pika helps the group get to “we’re doing this” with less friction, less nudging, and fewer vague replies.

- Specific observations about real group behavior
- Warm jokes rooted in recognition

## Internal creative brief

### What the audience should feel
- Seen
- Relieved
- Slightly amused
- Optimistic that planning can be less annoying

### What the audience should understand
- Pika is for group decisions
- It is lightweight, not heavy-duty project management
- It helps a group get unstuck and move faster
- It is useful in everyday planning moments, not just formal events

### What makes the message distinct
Most planning tools sound managerial. Pika should sound social, observant, and tuned to the weirdly universal problem of casual group indecision.

### Single-minded takeaway
**Pika helps groups stop circling and actually choose something.**

### Creative direction notes
- Keep everything concrete
- Use examples people instantly recognize
- Stay playful, but never cute at the expense of clarity
- Write like someone who has personally suffered through planless group chats
- Every line should feel like it belongs to the same smart, warm voice
