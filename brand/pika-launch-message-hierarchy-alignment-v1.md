## 1) Top-line framing

### Primary framing
**APPROVED**

- **Message role:** top-line brand promise
- **Headline:** Pika keeps the game moving.
- **Subhead:** A social game flow designed for instant restarts, faster rematches, and less dead time between rounds.
- **Use on:** landing hero, waitlist hero, deck cover, one-pager opener, share card
- **Intent:** establish momentum and replay value in plain language

### Secondary framing options
**APPROVED**

#### Option A
- **Headline:** Less waiting. More rematches.
- **Subhead:** Pika helps groups get back into the game without the usual reset drag.
- **Use on:** paid tests, social posts, compact hero layouts, referral modules

#### Option B
- **Headline:** Start fast. Restart faster.
- **Subhead:** Built for the moment when the round ends and everyone wants another one.
- **Use on:** deck section breaks, product explainer blocks, short-form placements

#### Option C
- **Headline:** The round ended. Not the momentum.
- **Subhead:** Pika is designed to keep the room warm between rounds and ready for the next matchup.
- **Use on:** narrative slides, email headers, launch copy variations

### Audience emphasis
**PROVISIONAL**

- **Primary audience emphasis:** all players in social groups
- **Secondary audience emphasis:** hosts coordinating the room
- **Open variable:** whether growth surfaces should target hosts more aggressively than shared group value

## 3) Proof placeholder structure

### Proof policy
**APPROVED**

- Keep approved messaging qualitative unless evidence exists.
- Pair any quantified or performance-sensitive message with a visible placeholder until proof is verified.
- If proof is missing, the claim block remains PROVISIONAL.

### Proof block template
**PROVISIONAL**

- **Claim status:** APPROVED / PROVISIONAL
- **Evidence needed:** [metric / quote / screenshot / internal observation / launch data]

- **Verification state:** [unverified / in review / approved]
- **Swap-ready proof line:** [insert externally safe proof statement]
- **Footnote if needed:** [insert note]

**APPROVED**

## 4) CTA logic

### Primary CTA system
**APPROVED**

| Surface type | Primary CTA | Message logic |
|---|---|---|
| Landing hero | Join the waitlist | Best default action when product access is not fully open |
| Deck / one-pager | Join the waitlist | Keeps launch ask simple and consistent |
| Share module | Share Pika | Best when user already understands the product |
| Invite module | Copy link | Best utility CTA when the goal is immediate sending |

### Secondary CTA system
**APPROVED**

| Surface type | Secondary CTA | Message logic |
|---|---|---|
| Landing hero | Learn more | Use only if supporting detail exists below the fold |
| Waitlist block | No secondary CTA or lightweight reassurance line | Reduce decision clutter |
| Share module | Copy link | Use as utility support to share prompt |
| Product explainer | Bring your group | Use when social context is the benefit |

### Waitlist CTA language
**APPROVED**

- Join the waitlist
- You’re in
- Try again

### Share CTA language
**APPROVED**

- Share Pika
- Copy link
- Bring your group

### Incentive CTA variants
**PROVISIONAL**

- Invite friends for earlier access
- Move up the list
- Bring your crew in faster

_Use only if the underlying reward or referral system is real._

### CTA sequencing rules
**APPROVED**

- Lead with one action, not multiple competing asks.
- Use waitlist CTAs on cold traffic surfaces.
- Use share CTAs after comprehension, confirmation, or excitement moments.
- Do not promise access speed, priority, or rewards unless confirmed.

## 5) Voice guardrails

### Approved voice rules
**APPROVED**

- Crisp over clever.
- Warm, not chatty.
- Specific, not theatrical.
- Keep the copy calm, quick, and obvious.
- Prefer plain language that sounds human, not branded performance poetry.

### Approved style signals
**APPROVED**

- Short sentences beat slogan soup.
- One strong idea per block.
- Social specificity is better than abstract product language.
- If the line could fit any startup, it is too generic.

### Language to avoid
**APPROVED**

- seamless
- robust
- best-in-class
- frictionless ecosystem
- revolutionary
- game-changing
- category-defining
- empower your users
- unlock engagement
- optimize social experiences

### Tone handling around excitement
**APPROVED**

- Celebrate momentum without overselling.
- Sound confident only where the product can support it.
- Keep “fun” grounded in the real social use case: replay energy, group flow, and rematch ease.

## 6) Claim guardrails

### Safe external claims
**APPROVED**

- Pika is built for instant restarts.
- Pika is designed to reduce restart friction.
- Pika keeps players moving from one round to the next.
- Pika is made for fast rematches.
- Pika is designed for replay-friendly group flow.

### Claims requiring proof before external use
**PROVISIONAL**

- Any statement with percentages, rankings, superlatives, or timing metrics
- Fastest
- Best
- Most social
- Everyone
- Never
- No setup
- Zero friction
- Players stay longer
- Improves retention
- Cuts restart time by [X]%
- Loved by hosts

### Confidence-language rules
**APPROVED**

- Use “designed to,” “built to,” or “made for” when speaking about intended outcomes.
- Do not imply certainty when the product has not proved it.
- If launch details, mechanics, or availability may change, mark related language PROVISIONAL.
- If a claim needs a footnote to stop being misleading, rewrite it or downgrade it.

## 8) Review notes for cross-team alignment

### Reuse guidance
**APPROVED**

- Growth may remix APPROVED lines by placement and length, but not by changing the strategic promise.
- Top-line framing should stay centered on momentum, restart ease, and replay flow.
- Share and waitlist systems should remain distinct: waitlist for commitment, share for amplification.

### Escalation triggers
**PROVISIONAL**

Escalate before publishing if any downstream asset introduces:

- quantified claims
- referral rewards
- launch timing promises
- comparative claims
- audience repositioning toward a materially different user

### Scope boundary
**APPROVED**

This package aligns message hierarchy only. It does not approve new strategy, new audience positioning, product UI copy, visual design direction, pricing language, or roadmap promises.
