# Pika Executable Surface Flow
_Status: board-ready integrated flow for landing, waitlist, and post-signup confirmation using the locked launch-support narrative._

## Canonical Narrative Baseline
Pika helps groups stop losing the plan in the chat by turning messy messages into one shareable plan everyone can check.

---

## Chosen Hierarchy
### Primary promise
Stop losing the plan in the chat.

### Supporting explanation
Pika turns messy group messages into one shareable plan everyone can check.

### Primary CTA route
Join the waitlist

### Post-signup share hook
Share Pika with the people you make plans with.

---

## 1) Landing Surface

### Goal
Make the value legible in a few seconds and move qualified visitors into the waitlist flow.

### Recommended content hierarchy
**Hero headline**
- Stop losing the plan in the chat.

**Hero support line**
- Pika turns messy group messages into one shareable plan everyone can check.

**Primary CTA**
- Join the waitlist

**Optional supporting line**
- Early access for people who plan in group chats.

### User takeaway
- “This is for group plans that get buried in messages.”
- “I do not need to understand the whole product to know why this matters.”

### Must-hold constraints
- Do not broaden into generic productivity language.
- Do not imply a fully mature platform beyond current credibility.
- Keep the promise anchored in chat chaos → shared plan clarity.

---

## 2) Waitlist Surface

### Goal
Convert interested visitors while preserving the same message hierarchy from landing.

### Recommended content hierarchy
**Headline**
- Get early access to Pika

**Support line**
- Stop losing the plan in the chat. Join the waitlist to try a simpler way to keep everyone on the same plan.

**Primary action**
- Join the waitlist

**Expectation-setting line**
- We’re opening access in stages.

### User takeaway
- “This is real, but early.”
- “The value is consistent with what I clicked from the landing page.”
- “Signing up is the right next step.”

### Must-hold constraints
- Form stays minimal.
- No incentive framing required to complete signup.
- No new promise introduced here that was not earned on landing.

---

## 3) Post-Signup Confirmation Surface

### Goal
Confirm success, reinforce the product promise, and present a believable optional share action.

### Recommended content hierarchy
**Confirmation headline**
- You’re on the list

**Confirmation support line**
- Thanks for joining. Pika helps groups stop losing the plan in the chat by turning messy messages into one shareable plan everyone can check.

**Optional share prompt**
- Share Pika with the people you make plans with.

**Optional share support line**
- If your group chats lose the plan too, send them this.

### User takeaway
- “I completed the action successfully.”
- “The product promise is reinforced.”
- “Sharing makes sense because it matches how I already coordinate plans.”

### Must-hold constraints
- Share prompt is optional and low-pressure.
- No spammy reward, leaderboard, or referral gimmick.
- Share language stays grounded in real planning behavior.

---

## End-to-End Surface Flow
### Step 1: landing
User arrives and sees:
- “Stop losing the plan in the chat.”
- “Pika turns messy group messages into one shareable plan everyone can check.”
- CTA: “Join the waitlist”

### Step 2: waitlist
After clicking through, user sees:
- “Get early access to Pika”
- “Stop losing the plan in the chat. Join the waitlist to try a simpler way to keep everyone on the same plan.”
- CTA: “Join the waitlist”
- “We’re opening access in stages.”

### Step 3: confirmation
After successful signup, user sees:
- “You’re on the list”
- Reinforced value statement
- Optional share prompt: “Share Pika with the people you make plans with.”
- Optional support: “If your group chats lose the plan too, send them this.”

## Initial Experiment Framing

### Experiment focus
Test whether the tighter chat-chaos framing drives stronger qualified conversion than a more descriptive but less immediate value presentation.

### Recommended primary test
**Variant A**
- Headline-first pain framing:
  - “Stop losing the plan in the chat.”

**Variant B**
- Slightly more explanatory framing:
  - “Turn messy group messages into one shareable plan everyone can check.”

### Constant elements
- Same CTA: “Join the waitlist”
- Same waitlist structure
- Same confirmation/share prompt structure
- Same expectation-setting that access is opening in stages

### Success readout
- Primary: landing visitor → waitlist signup conversion
- Secondary: landing CTA click-through, waitlist completion rate, post-signup share action rate

### Decision rule
- Prefer the route that improves waitlist conversion without creating a mismatch that hurts waitlist completion or makes the post-signup share action feel less believable.
