# U3 Messaging Lock

## Canonical Hook
**Build your room. Rank with friends.**

Use this exact top-line hook across waitlist, referral, and share-loop surfaces.

## Approved Supporting Framing Points
Use only the approved language below when extending the hook:

1. **Build your room**
2. **Rank with friends**
3. **Compare picks**
4. **See where everyone lands**
5. **Move up the waitlist by inviting people you actually want in the room**

When selecting supporting copy for a surface, pull from this exact language and do not introduce new positioning frames.

## Surface Adaptations

### 1) Waitlist
**Headline**  
Build your room. Rank with friends.

**Subhead**  
Build your room, rank with friends, and compare picks.

**Body**  
Join the waitlist to build your room, compare picks, and see where everyone lands.

**CTA**  
Join the waitlist

**Optional Secondary Line**  
Move up the waitlist by inviting people you actually want in the room.

### 2) Referral
**Headline**  
Build your room. Rank with friends.

**Subhead**  
Move up the waitlist by inviting people you actually want in the room.

**Body**  
Invite the people you want in your room so you can rank with friends, compare picks, and see where everyone lands.

**CTA**  
Invite your room

**Optional Secondary Line**  
Build your room, then move up the waitlist together.

### 3) Share-Loop
**Headline**  
Build your room. Rank with friends.

**Subhead**  
Compare picks. See where everyone lands.

**Body**  
Share your room with the people you actually want in it so you can rank with friends and compare picks together.

**CTA**  
Share your room

**Optional Secondary Line**  
Move up the waitlist by inviting people you actually want in the room.

## Usage Rules
- The canonical hook must appear first and unchanged: **Build your room. Rank with friends.**
- Supporting lines must stay within the approved framing points.
- If space is limited, keep the canonical hook and use one supporting line only.
- Referral and share-loop copy should reinforce real-friend participation, not broad or spammy distribution.
- Waitlist movement language should only appear where invite mechanics are present.

## Testing Guardrails
- Alternatives may only appear **below** the primary hook.
- Alternatives may not introduce a competing positioning frame.
- Do not replace the canonical hook with curiosity hooks, utility-only hooks, or generic social-planning language.
- Do not add incentive framing beyond: **move up the waitlist by inviting people you actually want in the room**.
- Do not use copy that shifts emphasis away from the product’s core loop: build your room, rank with friends, compare picks, see where everyone lands.

## Copy QA Checklist
- Primary hook is exact and unchanged.
- Supporting copy uses approved language only.
- Surface copy matches the intended mechanic: waitlist, referral, or share-loop.
- Invite language targets people the user actually wants in the room.
- No competing tagline, promise, or positioning frame appears above or alongside the hook.
