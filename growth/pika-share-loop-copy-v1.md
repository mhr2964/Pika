## Placement Notes

### Result screen
Use one result-driven hook as the headline or subhead, paired with a clear primary share CTA. Default recommendation:
- **Hook:** This result understood our friend group a little too well.
- **CTA:** Send this to the group

If the result feels more comedic or revealing, swap in a playful or punchy hook.

### Share prompt
Keep copy short and curiosity-led. Best options:
- Share your result
- Send this to the group
- Drop the reveal in chat

Use playful/punchy variants when the result itself is strong enough to carry the tone.

### Post-share / invite moments
Use lightweight, non-incentivized prompts that frame sharing as something to try with people the user already plans with. Default recommendation:
- Make one for your group

Alternates:
- Send it to the people you actually make plans with
- Try this with your planning crew
- See what your group gets

### Guardrails
- Do not imply rewards, credits, queue priority, faster access, or unlocks for sharing.
- Keep the framing social and observational, not absolute or inflated.
- Favor “group,” “plans,” “chat,” and “result” language over generic referral language.
