# Pika Launch User Flow + Success Framing
_Status: board-ready. Limited to currently credible near-launch surfaces._

## Recommended User Flow

### 1) Arrival: landing page
**User sees**
- Clear headline positioning Pika as a practical tool for turning social plans into something easier to coordinate
- Brief supporting copy focused on immediate usefulness, not full-platform completeness
- One primary CTA to join/get access
- Optional lightweight social proof or “early access” framing if already approved elsewhere

**User decision**
- Understand what Pika is for in a few seconds
- Decide whether the value is relevant enough to continue

**Primary desired action**
- Click the main CTA into the waitlist/join flow

**Risk to watch**
- Message is interesting but too vague
- Product feels unfinished or overpromised

---

### 2) Consideration: waitlist/join surface
**User sees**
- Simple join form with minimal friction
- Short reminder of the core value proposition
- Confirmation that access is early / near-launch, setting correct expectations

**User decision**
- Decide whether the promise is credible enough to exchange contact info

**Primary desired action**
- Complete waitlist signup

**Risk to watch**
- Friction from too many fields
- Unclear benefit for joining now
- Anxiety caused by unclear launch timing or product readiness

---

### 3) Conversion: confirmation state
**User sees**
- Immediate success confirmation
- Reinforcement that they are in / on the list
- Lightweight next step that feels natural, not forced

**User decision**
- Feel rewarded and clear on what happens next

**Primary desired action**
- Accept/share the confirmation state rather than dropping immediately

**Risk to watch**
- Confirmation feels like a dead end
- No momentum carried forward from signup completion

---

### 4) Amplification: share action
**User sees**
- Simple post-signup share option tied to the product’s social-planning usefulness
- Low-pressure prompt to send/copy/share with friends they would realistically plan with

**User decision**
- Decide whether sharing feels relevant and socially believable

**Primary desired action**
- Copy link / trigger share / use any currently available share mechanism

**Risk to watch**
- Share prompt feels generic, needy, or incentive-driven
- Users do not understand why they should share before the full product is live

---

## Recommended Flow Shape
**Landing page → Waitlist form → Confirmation state → Optional share prompt**

Why this flow:
- It matches currently credible surfaces
- It keeps the funnel short
- It avoids depending on full product depth that may not be launch-ready
- It creates one clean primary conversion and one optional amplification action

---

## Success Metric Framing

### Primary success metric
**Waitlist conversion rate**
- Definition: percentage of landing-page visitors who complete waitlist signup

**Why this is primary**
- Best indicator that the current messaging + product promise is believable enough to create near-launch demand

---

### Secondary success metrics
**Landing CTA click-through rate**
- Measures whether above-the-fold positioning creates enough interest to enter the join flow

**Waitlist completion rate**
- Measures whether the join surface introduces friction or fails to sustain intent

**Post-signup share action rate**
- Measures whether the product framing is socially legible enough that users willingly pass it along

**Shared-traffic conversion rate**
- Measures whether the share loop brings in similarly qualified interest rather than low-intent visits

---

## Hypothesis Framing

### Core hypothesis
If the landing page frames Pika as a credible, immediately understandable tool for making social planning easier, then qualified visitors will convert to the waitlist at a healthy rate without needing heavy incentives or overexplaining the full product.

### Funnel hypothesis
If the waitlist flow is short and expectation-setting is clear, then users who click into the join flow will complete signup rather than dropping due to uncertainty or friction.

### Share-loop hypothesis
If the post-signup share prompt is grounded in real social use (“share with the people you actually make plans with”), then a meaningful subset of new signups will take a share action without requiring spammy rewards.

---

## What “Good” Looks Like Qualitatively
- Visitors quickly understand what problem Pika helps with
- The experience feels early but real, not vague or hype-driven
- Joining feels like a sensible next step, not a commitment leap
- Sharing feels contextually natural after signup, not bolted on

---

## Key Failure Modes To Monitor
- Strong curiosity on landing page but weak CTA click-through
- Good click-through but weak waitlist completion
- Good signup conversion but near-zero share engagement
- Shared traffic arrives but does not convert, implying unclear or low-trust social framing
- Messaging creates interest only when overly broad, indicating weak believable positioning

---

## Board-Ready Summary
**Recommended flow:** landing page → waitlist signup → confirmation → optional share  
**Primary metric:** landing visitor to waitlist signup conversion  
**Supporting metrics:** CTA click-through, waitlist completion, share action rate, shared-traffic conversion  
**Core hypothesis:** believable near-launch positioning around easier social planning will convert better than broad or overbuilt product claims  
**Constraint:** keep all messaging and actions tied to currently credible surfaces only
