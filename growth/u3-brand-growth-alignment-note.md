## 1) Fixed Canonical Hook Rule

On launch-critical acquisition and referral surfaces, the top-line signal remains:

**Build your room. Rank with friends.**

This line stays fixed on:
- landing hero and primary landing entry points
- waitlist signup and confirmation states
- referral invite surfaces
- share-loop surfaces where the hierarchy can be preserved

Do not replace, outrank, or test against this line on primary launch surfaces.

---

## 2) Allowed Use of the Alternate Support Hook

The brand alternate line may be used only as subordinate support, never as competing top-line positioning on launch-critical surfaces.

Allowed use:
- secondary/subordinate copy blocks beneath the canonical hook
- lower-priority reinforcement lines
- constrained support-copy tests that do not displace the canonical hook
- non-primary placements where the user has already received the main frame

Not allowed:
- hero headline replacement
- waitlist module headline replacement
- referral-screen top-line replacement
- share-loop opening line replacement where the canonical hook can still fit

Operational rule: if the alternate line appears, it must read as supporting texture under the fixed growth hook, not as a second positioning system.

---

## 3) Surface-by-Surface Alignment Notes

### Landing
- Keep **Build your room. Rank with friends.** as the hero headline.
- Brand language may appear in the subhead, body, or reinforcement line if it supports room-building, ranking with friends, comparing picks, or seeing where everyone lands.
- Do not introduce the alternate line as a second headline or pre-head above the canonical hook.
- CTA remains within approved growth options, with `Join the waitlist` as the default launch-safe choice.

### Waitlist / Referral
- Keep **Build your room. Rank with friends.** as the module or page headline.
- Use approved growth framing for the signup and referral explanation:
  - build your room
  - rank with friends
  - compare picks
  - see where everyone lands
  - move up the waitlist by inviting people you actually want in the room
- Brand alternate language may be used only below the headline and only if it does not weaken waitlist clarity or referral mechanics.
- Referral explainer and CTA language should remain operational and direct; do not swap in softer brand copy where waitlist movement needs to be explicit.

### Share-Loop
- Lead with **Build your room. Rank with friends.** whenever the share format allows.
- In compressed formats, shorten supporting copy first before substituting any alternate brand line.
- Brand alternate language may appear as secondary message texture in longer share captions or body copy, but not as the opening line when the canonical hook fits.
- Keep invite action and compare-picks framing clear; do not let brand phrasing obscure the room/invite action.

---

## 4) Mismatch / Hierarchy Risks Requiring Attention

| Risk | Why It Matters | Required Handling |
|---|---|---|
| Alternate brand line appears above the canonical hook | Creates competing top-line positioning on launch-critical surfaces | Reorder so the canonical hook is first and most prominent |
| Alternate line used as a replacement headline on landing or waitlist | Breaks the approved messaging lock | Treat as off-brief and revert to the canonical hook |
| Share surfaces lead with brand texture instead of room/invite action | Weakens acquisition clarity in compressed formats | Keep the canonical hook first; compress supporting text instead |
| Referral copy becomes expressive but vague | Risks losing clear waitlist movement explanation | Preserve explicit referral language beneath the canonical hook |

## Operational Bottom Line
Brand expression can support the system, but growth’s launch-critical hierarchy does not change: the canonical hook stays first, the alternate line stays subordinate, and any surface that cannot maintain that order should be treated as a hierarchy risk.
