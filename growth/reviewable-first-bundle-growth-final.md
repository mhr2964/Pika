# PART I — WAITLIST MECHANICS SUMMARY

## FINAL BASELINE

### 1) Signup Flow
- Visitor lands on a waitlist surface that frames the product around clearer collaborative planning.
- Visitor submits email to join the waitlist.
- An optional field may collect what kinds of plans the user organizes most often.
- Signup language sets expectations around access updates and limited product-related communication.

### 2) Confirmation States
- After signup, the user sees a confirmation state indicating they are on the list.
- The confirmation state reinforces that the product becomes more useful when used with people the user already plans with.
- Confirmation includes a secondary invitation to share with collaborators or usual planning partners.

### 3) Referral Entry Points
- Primary referral/share entry point appears on the confirmation surface after waitlist signup.
- Share prompting may also appear in an invite or share module positioned immediately after confirmation or in adjacent lifecycle touchpoints.
- Referral/share language is framed as inviting collaborators into a shared planning workflow rather than promoting generic referral behavior.

### 4) Rank / Progress Logic
- Waitlist progression language should remain lightweight and legible.
- Any indication of position, movement, or progress should be presented as simple waitlist status context rather than gamified competition.
- Progress-related language should support clarity and motivation without shifting tone into leaderboard or reward-chasing behavior.

### 5) Incentive Framing
- Sharing is not framed around perks, prizes, or spam-style referral incentives.
- The motivating reason to share is collaborative usefulness: the product works better when the people involved in a plan can react in the same place.
- Referral context should emphasize “people you plan with” rather than broad audience expansion.

---

## PROVISIONAL SWAP ZONES

### A) Confirmation-State Handling
- Exact confirmation wording may shift based on whether the product surface shows a simple signup success state, early access queue language, or a more explicit waitlist-status module.
- If implementation exposes more visible waitlist status, copy should retain calm, utility-led framing instead of competitive framing.

### B) Rank / Progress Language Handling
- If rank, place, or progress UI is present, the copy should stay informational and low-hype.
- If no visible rank or progress UI is present, omit explicit progress language entirely rather than inventing motivational mechanics.

### C) Referral Surface Placement
- Share prompts may live directly in confirmation, in a dedicated invite module, or in a nearby follow-up surface.
- Placement can vary without changing the underlying framing principle: invite known collaborators because shared planning context improves usefulness.
