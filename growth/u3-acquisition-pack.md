# U3 Acquisition Pack

## 1) Landing-Page Message Structure

### Primary Hook
**Build your room. Rank with friends.**

This exact hook must appear as the top-line message.

### Supporting Structure
**Support line 1:** Build your room  
**Support line 2:** Rank with friends  
**Support line 3:** Compare picks  
**Support line 4:** See where everyone lands

### Hero Subhead
Build your room, rank with friends, compare picks, and see where everyone lands.

### Hero Body
Create your room with the people you actually want in it, then rank with friends and compare picks together.

### Primary CTA
Join the waitlist

### Secondary Supporting Line
Move up the waitlist by inviting people you actually want in the room.

### Below-the-Fold Reinforcement
- Build your room with your actual group
- Rank with friends instead of guessing alone
- Compare picks side by side
- See where everyone lands once everyone joins

### Usage Rules
- Keep **Build your room. Rank with friends.** as the first and most prominent line.
- Supporting copy may elaborate only with: build your room, rank with friends, compare picks, see where everyone lands, move up the waitlist by inviting people you actually want in the room.
- Do not introduce a competing frame about discovery, productivity, or generic social planning.

## 4) Lightweight Test Plan

### Scope
Test only:
- CTA language
- Referral-language clarity

Do not test:
- Replacement hooks
- Alternative positioning frames
- New product descriptions that compete with the primary message

### CTA Tests
**Control CTA:** Join the waitlist  
**Variant A:** Join your room  
**Variant B:** Invite your room

**Readout goal:** Determine which CTA best converts without weakening clarity around waitlist entry and room creation.

### Referral-Language Tests
**Control line:** Move up the waitlist by inviting people you actually want in the room.  
**Variant A:** Invite people you actually want in your room to move up the waitlist.  
**Variant B:** Your place can move up when the people you invite join the waitlist.

**Readout goal:** Improve comprehension of how waitlist movement works while preserving the same underlying mechanic.

### Test Guardrails
- The primary hook must remain unchanged and in the top position: **Build your room. Rank with friends.**
- Variants may appear only below the primary hook.
- Tests may refine CTA or referral explanation only.
- Do not introduce urgency, exclusivity, rewards, or generic viral language.
- Every referral test must clearly explain that waitlist movement is tied to inviting people the user actually wants in the room and those people joining the waitlist.

### Success Criteria
- Users understand that they join a waitlist to build a room and rank with friends.
- Users understand that waitlist movement comes from invited people joining.
- CTA changes do not create a competing frame or obscure the primary positioning.
