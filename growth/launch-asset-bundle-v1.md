# Launch Asset Bundle v1

Status: approved-baseline assembly with audience-fit resonance edits integrated  
Scope: landing, form, confirmation, referral/share surfaces, CTA variants, and provisional copy swaps  
Constraint: growth must emerge from the product’s expressive outputs and social planning use case, not spammy incentives or generic newsletter capture

---

## Bundle Intent

This bundle assembles the approved launch copy baseline into one implementation-ready artifact for a first-pass launch surface. It is designed to help early visitors quickly understand the product as a tool for turning loose ideas into plans people can actually react to, refine, and share with others.

Primary audience fit in this pass:
- people organizing trips, events, dinners, meetups, and collaborative plans
- people tired of fragmented planning across notes, chats, and half-finished docs
- people who want something clearer than “brainstorming,” but lighter than formal project software

Core messaging guardrails:
- emphasize expressive outputs and social coordination value
- avoid spammy referral energy, sweepstakes framing, or vague “join the newsletter” language
- keep tone useful, concrete, and lightly social
- make sharing feel like a natural next step in collaborative planning, not a growth hack

---

## Messaging Spine

### Core Promise
Turn rough planning ideas into something people can see, react to, and shape together.

### Product Framing
A lightweight planning workspace for ideas that need structure before they become real plans.

### Primary User Outcome
Instead of explaining a plan over and over across messages, users can share one clear version that others can respond to.

### Social Value
The product becomes more useful when a plan is easy to pass along to collaborators, guests, or friends who need context before they weigh in.

### Tone
- clear
- collaborative
- concrete
- low-hype
- socially useful rather than promotional

## Voice and Copy Rules

### Do
- speak to real planning scenarios
- mention collaboration and shared context
- make value legible before asking for signup
- keep sharing framed around usefulness with known collaborators
- prefer “people you plan with” over generic “friends” when possible

### Don’t
- imply rewards that do not exist
- overstate virality or momentum
- lean on founder-theater or hype language
- reduce the product to note-taking or generic productivity
- ask people to “spread the word” without clear user value
