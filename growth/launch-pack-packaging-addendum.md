# Executive Summary

Pika’s launch-readiness baseline is built around a product-led growth model for social planning. The core decision is to position Pika as the tool that turns vague group intent into one clear plan, especially for friend trips and other multi-person plans where coordination friction is high. The recommended v1 path is to launch with a simple funnel: first-touch conversion on a focused homepage, lightweight waitlist capture, immediate post-submit momentum, and utility-driven sharing that pulls real collaborators into the planning context.

The pack recommends avoiding generic newsletter framing, spammy incentives, and referral mechanics disconnected from actual use. Instead, the primary growth engine should be expressive outputs and collaborative planning artifacts that people naturally share with friends, tripmates, or other decision participants. In practice, v1 should prioritize the friend-group planning wedge, capture active planning intent in the waitlist flow, and use sharing prompts that improve the plan rather than merely promote the product.

The strongest v1 share behaviors are: sharing a result card, inviting real group members into a plan, and sending a planning snapshot that gives others something concrete to react to. Conversion copy should consistently emphasize reduced chaos, faster alignment, visible preferences, and clearer decisions. Launch sequencing should therefore favor users with imminent planning needs, multi-person contexts, and high likelihood of bringing in actual collaborators.

Several elements in the baseline are explicitly provisional because the approved pack was assembled from partial upstream context. Downstream teams should treat the strategic structure as approved, while validating any exact wording or implementation assumptions flagged as provisional before public launch.
